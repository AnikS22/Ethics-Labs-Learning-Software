# Ethics Lab Courses Summary

## Overview

Ethics Lab offers two comprehensive courses designed to provide both theoretical understanding and practical skills in AI ethics.

## Course 1: AI Ethics

**Focus**: Understanding AI ethics and philosophical questions

### Course Details
- **Title**: AI Ethics
- **Type**: Theoretical/Philosophical
- **Level**: Introductory (No prerequisites)
- **Duration**: 8 Chapters, 28 Lessons
- **Certification**: Yes

### What Students Learn
- Foundational concepts in AI ethics and philosophy
- Understanding bias, fairness, and discrimination in AI
- Privacy, surveillance, and data rights
- Autonomy, agency, and responsibility in AI systems
- Deep philosophical questions about AI consciousness and moral status
- Social impact, justice, and democratic implications
- Real-world case studies and ethical analysis

### Target Audience
- Students and academics
- Professionals interested in AI ethics
- Policymakers
- Concerned citizens
- No technical background required

### Course Structure
1. Foundations of AI Ethics (3 lessons)
2. Bias and Fairness (3 lessons)
3. Privacy and Surveillance (3 lessons)
4. Autonomy and Agency (3 lessons)
5. Philosophical Questions (4 lessons)
6. Social Impact and Justice (3 lessons)
7. Case Studies and Applications (4 lessons)
8. Ethics in Practice (3 lessons)

---

## Course 2: Ethical Creation of AI

**Focus**: Learning how to make and develop AI ethically

### Course Details
- **Title**: Ethical Creation of AI
- **Type**: Practical/Technical
- **Level**: Intermediate (Basic ML knowledge recommended)
- **Duration**: 10 Chapters, 33 Lessons
- **Certification**: Yes

### What Students Learn
- Ethics by design principles and development methodologies
- Ethical data collection, management, and governance
- Fairness-aware machine learning algorithms
- Transparency and explainability techniques
- Privacy-preserving AI methods (differential privacy, federated learning)
- Robustness, safety, and security in AI systems
- Accountability mechanisms and governance frameworks
- Human-centered design for AI
- Deployment, monitoring, and incident response
- Practical tools and frameworks for ethical AI development

### Target Audience
- AI developers
- Data scientists
- ML engineers
- Technical professionals
- Software engineers working with AI

### Course Structure
1. Introduction to Ethical AI Development (3 lessons)
2. Data Ethics and Management (3 lessons)
3. Fairness in Machine Learning (3 lessons)
4. Transparency and Explainability (3 lessons)
5. Privacy-Preserving AI (4 lessons)
6. Robustness and Safety (3 lessons)
7. Accountability and Governance (3 lessons)
8. Human-Centered Design (3 lessons)
9. Deployment and Monitoring (3 lessons)
10. Tools and Frameworks (3 lessons)

## Complementary Learning Path

These courses are designed to complement each other:

1. **Start with AI Ethics** (if new to the field)
   - Builds foundational understanding
   - Develops critical thinking skills
   - Provides philosophical context

2. **Then take Ethical Creation of AI** (for technical implementation)
   - Applies ethical principles in practice
   - Provides technical tools and methods
   - Enables responsible AI development

3. **Or take both simultaneously**
   - Theory and practice reinforce each other
   - Comprehensive understanding
   - Well-rounded expertise
